01 — The Premise

When attention stops scaling

The Transformer has been the workhorse of modern AI, but its quadratic self-attention and ever-growing key–value (KV) cache are increasingly at odds with the workloads that now define the field: million-token contexts, persistent memory, long-horizon agentic execution, and reasoning that consumes large amounts of test-time compute.

Beyond Transformers is a workshop on the emerging generation of sub-quadratic and post-Transformer architectures — state-space models, linear and gated-linear attention, modern recurrent networks, hybrids, and non-autoregressive / diffusion language models — that promise to break the quadratic barrier while matching, or exceeding, Transformer quality. These models moved from research curiosity to production reality in barely two years (Mamba, xLSTM, RWKV, gated linear attention, Jamba, Nemotron-H), yet the community working on them is fragmented across architecture, training, systems, hardware, and theory.

i

Architecture

Sub-quadratic primitives that retain in-context learning, recall, and expressivity.

ii

Training & Conversion

Pre-training new architectures efficiently and distilling Transformers into sub-quadratic ones.

iii

Systems & Hardware

Kernels, serving, and accelerators tuned for recurrent / state-centric computation.

iv

Evaluation & Theory

Compute-aware benchmarks and a principled understanding of capacity–recall trade-offs.

Why this, why now?

The last two years marked an inflection point. Sub-quadratic models progressed from small-scale proofs of concept to architectures that match Transformers at multi-billion-parameter scale — and hybrids now ship in production systems from major industrial labs. Demand has outrun the Transformer, credible alternatives have matured, and the field is fragmenting across SSM, linear-attention, diffusion-LM, systems, and hardware efforts with non-comparable baselines. A NeurIPS workshop is the natural place to coordinate this agenda now, while the design space is still open.

04 — Open Call

Eight threads, one agenda

We invite papers and demos across the full lifecycle — architecture, training, conversion, inference, systems, hardware, and evaluation. Topics include, but are not limited to:

Sub-Quadratic & Recurrent Architectures

  • State-space models and selective/structured SSMs (S4, Mamba family)
  • Linear, kernelized & gated-linear attention; modern RNNs (xLSTM, RWKV, DeltaNet)
  • Structured-matrix and convolutional token mixers as attention replacements
  • Expressivity, recall & length generalization of non-attention models

Hybrid & Modular Architectures

  • Interleaving attention with sub-quadratic layers — how much attention is needed?
  • Mixture-of-Experts & sparse routing with sub-quadratic backbones
  • Modular, many-in-one models that adapt to hardware and budgets

Non-Autoregressive & Diffusion LMs

  • Diffusion and masked/parallel decoding for faster, scalable generation
  • Trade-offs between autoregressive and non-autoregressive paradigms

Long-Context & Memory-Centric Models

  • Million-token context, persistent memory & retrieval-aware architectures
  • KV-cache elimination, compression & bounded-state designs
  • Architectures for agentic, long-horizon, and stateful execution

Efficient Training, Distillation & Conversion

  • Efficient pre-training of new and hybrid architectures
  • Distilling / up-cycling Transformers into sub-quadratic models
  • Parameter-efficient fine-tuning for emerging architectures

Inference, Systems & Hardware Co-Design

  • Hardware-aware kernels & serving for recurrent/state-centric compute
  • Speculative decoding & quantization for non-Transformer / hybrid models
  • Accelerator, memory-hierarchy & edge/low-power co-design

Reasoning & Test-Time Compute

  • Reasoning & adaptive test-time compute with sub-quadratic backbones
  • Allocating “thinking time” without incurring quadratic cost

Evaluation, Benchmarking & Theory

  • Compute-aware evaluation: latency/quality, memory & energy trade-offs
  • Benchmarks, datasets & leaderboards for long-context models
  • Theory of state-space, linear-attention & recurrent models
